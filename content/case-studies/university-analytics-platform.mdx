## The Challenge

The university's data team was producing weekly engagement reports for 12 academic departments — each requiring manual data extraction from three separate systems, reconciliation in Excel, and a two-day turnaround from data pull to delivery.

As student numbers grew and leadership asked for more granular, real-time insight, the existing process couldn't scale.

## Our Approach

We started with a two-week discovery phase: mapping every data source, understanding how each department consumed the reports, and identifying which metrics actually drove decisions versus which were produced out of habit.

The output was a clear specification for a streaming analytics pipeline with a department-facing dashboard layer.

### Technical Architecture

We built a Python-native stack that the university's in-house team could own and extend:

- **Ingestion layer:** FastAPI service consuming events from the university's LMS, VLE, and library systems
- **Processing:** Pandas and Polars pipelines running on a scheduled job executor
- **Storage:** PostgreSQL with a denormalised reporting schema optimised for the dashboard queries
- **Visualisation:** Plotly Dash application with role-based access (department heads see only their data)

### Delivery

The platform was built in eight weeks across three phases: data pipeline, dashboard MVP, and stakeholder training. We ran weekly review sessions with the data team throughout to ensure the implementation matched operational reality.

## Results

The platform replaced the two-day manual process with a live dashboard updated every four hours. Department heads can now self-serve their own reports, slice by cohort, and export data directly — without a request to the central data team.

The university has since expanded the platform to cover three additional faculties not in the original scope.
